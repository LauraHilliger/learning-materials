Workshop in a Box: the 7 Shifts

The 7 Shifts are a set of stars, which guide Greenpeace in the 21st century. This Workshop in a Box is designed to support the Greenpeace community in embodying the new story of Greenpeace and implementing the 7 Shifts in everything from communications to campaign planning.

Made by: Laura Hilliger and the Greenpeace Story Team

Description

This Workshop in a Box is designed to inspire and empower Greenpeace staff and community as they begin living the new story of Greenpeace. As well as introducing them to the 7 Shifts and telling them more about what the new story of Greenpeace means in our work, we aim to build trust and confidence in live events and demystify participatory facilitation techniques.

We hope by the end of the workshop all participants will be equipped and excited about designing their work plans, events and campaigns using the 7 Shifts and the new story of Greenpeace.

Over the course of the training, participants will learn how to see themselves as part of the Greenpeace global community. They will learn to build plans and activities for specific audiences. The Story team supports facilitators in creating their own story based agendas and in documenting their work.

Learning objectives

By the end of the workshops, participants will be able to:

  • use the 7 Shifts in support of their ongoing social action
  • facilitate participatory experiences
  • run events, to share their learning with fellow Greenpeace peers and wider communities
  • identify the 7 Shifts and explain their individual intentions
  • give/receive constructive criticism
  • use critique to improve their work
  • collaborate and share online in a responsible way
  • understand and explain to others how to live the 7 Shifts
  • support Greenpeace in developing plans, campaigns, online and social activities

The participants will understand that:

  • they’re part of a global network and community that can help promote local initiatives
  • they’re key ambassadors for Greenpeace, both online and in their communities
  • learning more digital skills can help them, their peers and other communities to support and promote social action projects
  • collaborative participatory learning experiences are about community and learner focused design
  • facilitating and pacing the event is essential to sustaining participant engagement and energy levels
  • we have cultural notions of what learning is supposed to look like
  • modeling behavior we want to see in the world is an integral part of living the new story of Greenpeace
  • we can add the teaching and learning of the 7 Shifts into any campaign or topic
  • working openly means you release early and often to improve your own practice
  • living the new story intermingles social skills, technical skills and topical knowledge
  • being part of a community means you can improve your practice and help improve other’s as well
  • inviting people into your work is a good way to give them agency to contribute

What we'll make together

We'll use Mozilla's Webmaker tools to help campaigners think about their local initiatives and support them to understand how their creative energy can spur innovation within their network. We'll make:

  • An X-Ray Goggles remix of a site to include initial thoughts on event type, target audience, logistics and potential partners.
  • An event invitation and promotional page.
  • A video about what participants have learned about facilitation.
  • A customized Agenda with several activities and supporting media.
  • Creation of usable content and assets for promoting Greenpeace

Preparation

You need a large room that can comfortably fit all your participants in a circle. Small break out rooms are a plus. Optional: a projector to show demos.

It is also recommended to have a team of co-facilitators who can help shape and run the agenda with you. We recommend 1 facilitator to 5 participants.

Assessment and review

  • Discussion questions:
    • How can your work connect to the 7 Shifts and living the new story?
    • How can you adapt a resource and open it to others, and different people in your community? How can you hack your learning space to inspire more open learning?
    • How can remix invite learning?
    • Why is it important to connect to global communities?
    • How does communication online differ from communication offline?
    • What excited you the most about this training?
    • the least?
    • Sharing. Have you posted your work somewhere? Did you tweet with the #courageis hashtag? Have you attended a Story Community Call?

Assessment criteria

Did the learner:
  • participate?
  • collaborate?
  • help others?
  • outline a reasonable campaign plan?
  • make attempts to share outputs?
  • try to help others and make things?
